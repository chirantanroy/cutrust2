Section 01

Who We Are

Credit Union Pension & Benefits Trust ("CUtrust", "we", "us", or "our") is an umbrella entity comprising two core programs serving credit union employees and their families across British Columbia:

  • BC Credit Union Employees' Pension Plan — a multi-employer registered pension plan governed by a Board of Trustees, registered under the Pension Benefits Standards Act (BC) and the Pension Benefits Standards Act, 1985 (Canada). The Plan provides defined benefit and money purchase retirement, death, and termination benefits.
  • BC Credit Union Employee Benefits Trust — a multi-employer employee life and health trust continued under the Income Tax Act (Canada) effective January 1, 2021. The Trust delivers comprehensive employment benefits including life insurance, disability coverage, extended health, dental, vision, EFAP+, virtual wellness, family building benefits, and spending accounts.

CUtrust is based in Vancouver, British Columbia, and has designated a Privacy Officer responsible for overseeing compliance with this Policy and applicable privacy legislation. See Section 14 for contact details.

Section 02

Scope & Applicable Law

This Privacy Policy applies to personal information collected through the cutrust.com website (the "Website"), including contact forms, the AI-powered assistant ("Ask CUtrust"), LinkedIn feed content, and other digital interactions initiated through the Website.

What This Policy Does NOT Cover

  • Personal information held in pension plan administration systems — such as contribution histories, service records, and defined benefit entitlements — operated by TELUS Health on behalf of CUtrust
  • Benefits claims, health information, dental or drug records, disability claims, or any coverage records — administered by GreenShield Administration / HRoffice on behalf of CUtrust
  • Information held in member portals or employer portals operated by CUtrust's plan service providers
  • Personal information collected directly by participating credit union employers from their own employees

Applicable Legislation

  • BC PIPA — British Columbia's Personal Information Protection Act, SBC 2003, c. 63. The primary applicable law for personal information collected within BC by private-sector organizations, including trusts, not-for-profits, and benefit plan administrators.
  • PIPEDA — Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act. Applies to the extent personal information crosses provincial or national borders in the course of CUtrust's activities.
  • Sector-specific legislation — the Pension Benefits Standards Act (BC), the Income Tax Act (Canada), and related regulations impose specific obligations regarding the handling of pension and benefits records.

Section 04

Purposes for Collection

CUtrust collects personal information through the Website for the following purposes, which a reasonable person would consider appropriate in the circumstances:

  • Responding to enquiries — to respond to questions or requests from plan members, beneficiaries, employer representatives, or prospective participants about the Pension Plan, Benefits Trust programs, or employer administration
  • Improving the Website — to understand how visitors use the Website, including which pension and benefits resources are most accessed, in order to improve content and navigation
  • AI assistant quality assurance — to review and improve the accuracy and helpfulness of the Ask CUtrust assistant's responses about pension plan divisions, benefits coverage types, and program administration
  • Security and fraud prevention — to detect, investigate, and prevent unauthorized, fraudulent, or illegal activity
  • Legal and regulatory compliance — to meet obligations under applicable pension, benefits, and privacy legislation

We will identify the purpose for collecting personal information at or before the time of collection and will not collect information beyond what is reasonably necessary for those purposes.

Section 05

How We Use Your Information

Personal information collected through the Website will be used only for the purposes for which it was collected, or for consistent purposes, or as required or authorized by law. We will not use your personal information for marketing or promotional communications without your express consent.

CUtrust does not sell personal information to any third party. As a not-for-profit trust operating exclusively for the benefit of credit union members and employers, CUtrust has no commercial advertising relationships. Website usage data is used in aggregated and anonymized form to improve the site and is not used to build individual profiles for commercial purposes.

Section 06

Disclosure to Third Parties

CUtrust does not sell, rent, or trade personal information. We may disclose personal information to the following categories of third parties, only to the extent necessary for the purposes described in this Policy:

Plan Administration Service Providers

CUtrust works with contracted service providers who assist in administering the Pension Plan and Benefits Trust programs. These include:

  • TELUS Health (HRoffice) — pension plan employer and member administration; Employer Portal operations
  • GreenShield Administration / HRoffice — benefits plan employer and member administration, including enrolment, billing, claims management for health, dental, disability, and life insurance programs
  • Teladoc Health — virtual healthcare, mental wellness support, and Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) platform for eligible benefits members
  • Perkopolis — employee perks, discounts, and wellness platform for eligible benefits members
  • Actuarial, investment management, legal, audit, and consulting service providers engaged from time to time in support of both programs

Technology & Website Service Providers

  • Website hosting provider — for hosting and maintaining the Website infrastructure
  • Analytics provider — for anonymized/aggregated website traffic analysis (see Section 11)
  • AI technology provider — powering the Ask CUtrust assistant. Query data is processed subject to appropriate data processing agreements; your queries are not used to train third-party AI models without authorization

Legal and Regulatory Disclosure

We may disclose personal information without your consent where required or authorized by law, including to: comply with a valid court order, subpoena, or legal process; respond to a lawful request from the BC Financial Services Authority (pension plan regulator), the Canada Revenue Agency, or the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions; or investigate or prevent fraud, security threats, or abuse of the Website. Where permitted by law, we will make reasonable efforts to notify you before disclosing your information in response to a legal request.

All contracted service providers are required to use personal information only for the purposes for which it was disclosed and to protect it with appropriate safeguards consistent with PIPA.

Data Protection

Security & Retention

CUtrust implements reasonable administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect personal information collected through the Website against unauthorized access, collection, use, disclosure, modification, loss, or destruction.

Our Safeguards Include

HTTPS (TLS) encryption across all Website communications · Access controls limiting data access to authorized staff on a need-to-know basis · Regular security assessments and monitoring · Staff training on privacy obligations, data handling practices, and breach protocols · Contractual security requirements for third-party service providers handling personal information on our behalf

In the event of a material breach of security that creates a real risk of significant harm to individuals, CUtrust will notify affected individuals and the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner for British Columbia (OIPC), in accordance with applicable legal requirements under PIPA.

Retention

How Long We Keep Your Information

CUtrust retains personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. Once no longer required, personal information is securely destroyed, erased, or rendered anonymous.

Type of Information Retention Period
Contact form submissions (pension, benefits, or general enquiries) Up to 2 years from submission, or until the enquiry is resolved
Website analytics data Up to 26 months; aggregated data may be retained indefinitely
AI assistant interaction logs (quality assurance) Up to 12 months; earlier deletion available upon request
Server and security logs Up to 12 months, or longer if required for a security incident or legal matter
Information related to a legal matter or regulatory complaint Duration of the matter plus any applicable limitation period under BC law

Pursuant to PIPA, personal information used to make a decision that directly affects an individual will be retained for at least one year from the date of that decision, to allow a reasonable opportunity to access it.

Section 09

Your Rights Under PIPA

Under British Columbia's Personal Information Protection Act, you have the following rights with respect to personal information CUtrust holds about you through the Website:

Your RightWhat It Means
Right to Access You may request access to personal information CUtrust holds about you and receive an explanation of how it has been used and disclosed. We will respond within 30 days of a complete written request.
Right to Correct If you believe personal information we hold about you is inaccurate or incomplete, you may request that it be corrected or annotated. We will respond within 30 days.
Right to Withdraw Consent You may withdraw your consent to the collection, use, or disclosure of your personal information at any time, subject to legal and contractual restrictions. Note that withdrawal cannot be retroactive.
Right to Challenge Compliance You may challenge CUtrust's compliance with PIPA by contacting our Privacy Officer. If unsatisfied with the response, you may escalate to the OIPC (see Section 15).

How to Make a Privacy Request

Submit your request in writing to our Privacy Officer at info@cutrust.com or by mail to 908–1166 Alberni Street, Vancouver, BC V6E 3Z3. Please include sufficient information to verify your identity and describe the information or action requested. We will respond within 30 days of receiving a complete request, or notify you if additional time is required under PIPA.

Section 10

Cross-Border Transfers

CUtrust's primary operations and data storage are located in Canada. However, some of our third-party service providers — including cloud infrastructure providers and AI technology providers powering the Ask CUtrust assistant — may process or store data in jurisdictions outside of Canada, including the United States.

Where personal information is transferred outside of British Columbia or Canada, CUtrust takes reasonable steps to ensure that the recipient provides a comparable level of protection through contractual data processing agreements consistent with PIPA. You should be aware that personal information transferred outside Canada may be subject to access by foreign governments, courts, or law enforcement agencies under the laws of that jurisdiction.

Section 11

Cookies & Analytics

The Website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to improve the user experience, understand how the Website is used, and support its technical operation.

Types of Cookies

  • Strictly Necessary: Required for the Website to function. These cannot be disabled without affecting core site functionality (e.g., session management, security).
  • Analytics: Used to collect anonymized information about how visitors use the Website — which pension and benefits pages are most visited, navigation patterns, and time on site. This data is aggregated and does not identify you individually.
  • Functionality: Used to remember preferences you have set.

Managing Cookies

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can choose to block or delete cookies, though doing so may affect the functionality of the Website. You may also opt out of analytics tracking by using your browser's privacy settings or a relevant opt-out extension. Refer to your browser's help documentation for instructions.

Section 12

Children's Privacy

We do not knowingly collect personal information from individuals under 18 through the public Website. If you believe a minor has submitted personal information, please contact our Privacy Officer immediately so we may take appropriate steps.
