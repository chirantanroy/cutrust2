Pension Payment Information

Understanding how and when your pension payments are processed ensures you can plan your finances with confidence.

Payment Schedule

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. Your pension payments are administered and distributed by our custodian, RBC Investor & Treasury Services. All direct deposits to your bank account are processed through RBC's secure payment system.

Direct Deposit Setup

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. All pension payments are made via direct deposit to your designated Canadian bank account. To set up or change your banking information, please complete and submit the appropriate form to the CUtrust office.

We recommend keeping your banking details current to avoid any interruptions to your monthly pension income.

Stay Up to Date

Managing Your Account

Keep your personal information current to ensure uninterrupted pension payments and timely communications.

Change of Address

Moving to a new home? It's important to notify CUtrust of any address changes to ensure you continue to receive important correspondence, including tax slips and annual pension statements.

You can update your address by:

  • Logging into the Member Portal
  • Calling our pension support line at 1.844.451.9698
  • Emailing pensions@cutrust.com

Banking Information Updates

If you change your bank account, it's essential to update your banking details promptly to avoid any disruption to your pension deposits.

To update your banking information:

  • Complete a new Direct Deposit Authorization form
  • Attach a void cheque or official bank letter
  • Submit the form to the CUtrust office

Important: Please allow up to one full payment cycle for banking changes to take effect. We recommend submitting updates at least 30 days before your next expected payment.

Income Tax Withholding

You may request to adjust the amount of income tax withheld from your monthly pension payment. By default, tax is deducted based on CRA guidelines.

To request a change, submit a completed TD1 form (Federal) and TD1BC form (Provincial) to CUtrust. Changes generally take effect within one pay cycle.

Important Notifications

Reporting Life Changes

Certain life events can affect your pension benefits. It's crucial to inform CUtrust of these changes as soon as possible.

Marital Status Changes

Marriage, separation, divorce, or the death of a spouse can significantly impact your pension benefits, including survivor benefits and beneficiary designations.

Please notify CUtrust immediately when any marital status change occurs. You may need to provide:

  • Marriage certificate or legal separation/divorce documents
  • Updated beneficiary designation forms
  • Spousal consent documentation (if applicable)

Beneficiary Updates

Your beneficiary designation determines who will receive benefits in the event of your passing. It is important to review your beneficiary information periodically, especially after major life events.

Contact CUtrust to request a Beneficiary Designation Change form. Ensure all forms are signed, witnessed, and submitted to the pension office for processing.

Proof of Life / Pension Continuance

Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipiscing elit. CUtrust may periodically require retirees to confirm their continued eligibility for pension payments through a Proof of Life verification.

This process helps protect the integrity of the pension plan and ensures benefits are distributed correctly. Please respond promptly to any verification requests received by mail or email.

Your Well-Being

Wellness in Retirement

Retirement is a significant life transition. Taking care of your mental health and staying connected are key to a fulfilling retirement.

Mental Health Support

Transitioning to retirement can bring unexpected emotional challenges. It's perfectly normal to experience feelings of loss, anxiety, or a shift in identity after leaving the workforce.

  • BC Crisis Centre — 24/7 helpline: 1-800-784-2433
  • HereToHelp BC — Mental health and substance use information
  • BounceBack BC — Free guided self-help for adults 15+
  • Government of Canada Mental Health Resources

Staying Physically Active

Regular physical activity is one of the most important things you can do for your health as you age. Even moderate exercise can improve mood, energy, and overall well-being.

  • ParticipACTION — Guidelines for Older Adults
  • Local community centres often offer senior-specific fitness classes, including yoga, tai chi, swimming, and walking groups
  • BC Recreation and Parks Association programs for seniors

Social Connection & Volunteering

Staying socially engaged is crucial for mental well-being. Retirement is a wonderful time to explore new interests, connect with your community, and give back.

  • Volunteer Canada — Find volunteer opportunities near you
  • Local seniors' centres and community groups
  • Meetup — Join groups based on hobbies and interests
  • University of the Third Age (U3A) — Lifelong learning programs
